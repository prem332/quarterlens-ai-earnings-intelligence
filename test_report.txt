## Executive Summary
Apple reported FY2025 Q3 total net sales of $94.0 billion, up 10% year over year, with nine-month sales up 6% to $313.7 billion [FILING]. Growth was led by iPhone (+13% in the quarter), Services (+13%), and Mac (+15%), while iPad and Wearables, Home and Accessories declined in the quarter [FILING]. Gross margin remained resilient at 46.5% in the quarter, slightly above 46.3% a year ago, despite tariff pressure on product margins [FILING]. Regional performance was broad-based, with Europe and Greater China up in the quarter [FILING]. [UNVERIFIED]

## Key Financial Metrics (verified)
- Total net sales: $94,036 million, +10% YoY in the quarter; $313,695 million, +6% in the first nine months [FILING].
- iPhone net sales: $44,582 million, +13% quarterly; $160,561 million, +4% YTD [FILING].
- Mac net sales: $8,046 million, +15% quarterly; $24,982 million, +12% YTD [FILING].
- iPad net sales: $6,581 million, -8% quarterly; $21,071 million, +7% YTD [FILING].
- Wearables, Home and Accessories: $7,404 million, -9% quarterly; $26,673 million, -5% YTD [FILING].
- Services net sales: $27,423 million, +13% quarterly; $80,408 million, +13% YTD [FILING].
- Total gross margin: $43,718 million, up from $39,678 million; gross margin rate 46.5% versus 46.3% [FILING].
- Products gross margin rate: 34.5%, down from 35.3%; Services gross margin rate: 75.6%, up from 74.0% [FILING].
- Quarterly R&D expense: $8,866 million, versus $8,006 million a year ago [FILING].
- Quarterly SG&A expense: $6,650 million, versus $6,320 million a year ago [FILING].
- Capital return: $21.0 billion of common stock repurchased and $3.9 billion paid in dividends/dividend equivalents in the quarter [FILING].

## Guidance & Language Shifts
Management did not provide formal revenue or EPS guidance in the cited filing excerpts [FILING]. The company reiterated that cash, cash equivalents, marketable securities, operating cash flow, and debt-market access should be sufficient to fund cash requirements and the capital return program “over the next 12 months and beyond” [FILING]. Apple also highlighted ongoing seasonality and launch timing effects, noting that new product and service introductions can materially affect sales, costs, and channel inventory [FILING]. The quarter included announcements of iOS 26, macOS Tahoe 26, iPadOS 26, watchOS 26, visionOS 26, and tvOS 26 [FILING].

## Risk Factor Changes
Apple emphasized tariffs as a key operating risk, stating that new U.S. tariffs and reciprocal measures could materially affect business, results, supply chain, raw materials availability, pricing, and gross margin [FILING]. The filing also expanded macro risk framing around inflation, interest rates, and currency fluctuations, which “have directly and indirectly impacted, and could in the future materially impact” operations and financial condition [FILING]. Trade and other international disputes were cited as potential drivers of weaker consumer spending and sentiment [FILING]. Gross margin commentary explicitly noted that product margin was partially offset by tariffs in both the quarter and first nine months [FILING].

## Sentiment Overview
Operational tone was constructive, with broad-based revenue growth, stable consolidated gross margin, and double-digit Services expansion [FILING]. Mix remains supportive: Services grew faster than total company sales and continued to carry structurally higher margins than Products [FILING]. Counterbalancing that strength, Apple’s disclosure language remains cautious on tariffs, FX, and macro demand sensitivity, and Greater China was softer on a nine-month basis despite a better quarter [FILING]. The effective tax rate increased in the quarter due primarily to foreign currency revaluations of unrecognized tax benefits, adding another source of earnings variability [FILING].
